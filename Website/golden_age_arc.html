Golden Age Arc

Guts is a Swordsman who is haunted by Monsters. A brand leads the demons to him. At nighttime they materialize and try to take his life. He wonders the land with one Task in mind: slay an organization named "God Hand", who are responsible for his brand. The organization’s Leaders consist of five members thumb is occupied by Void, index finger by Femto, middle finger by Slan, ring finger by Ubik, and pinky finger by Conrad. When Guts is finally standing face to face with the group again, however, he fails miserably. With shear luck he gets away in one piece. During the encounter one thing became apparent, with one of the members of the Hand Guts clearly has more history, as he became very emotional at the sight of him in particular. The Author ends that chapter with his traumatized protagonist reminiscing over the past. And starts the next one with the sight of a tree, which has bodies hanging from its branches instead of leaves. A mercenary group holds next to it because one of their women is giving birth to a small child at the foot of the tree. The child born was Guts

Golden Age Arc I “The cursed Child”

Short after his birth, Guts mother passed away leaving him alone with his ‘good for nothing father in a group consisting of Mercs, criminals and low lifes. In his childhood guts endures abuse and loss, culminating in the killing of his adoptive father, Gambino, in self-defense. The consciences of Gambinos murder left Guts no other choice but to flee the mercenary group, for they wanted the little boys head as a price for Gambinos. Beaten up and exhausted he escapes them only to be faced with yet another pursuer. In yet another act of self-defense he kills a pack of wolves. He doesn’t know why he is alive, but he is not able to give into death, his body won’t let him, so he asks himself, why, what is his purpose in Life.

Golden Age Arc II "The Band of the Hawks"

Four years later his path crosses with Griffith after a battle where Guts defeats a formidable opponent. Griffith, intrigued by Guts' strength, captures and challenges guts to a dual for his freedom, which Guts loses. As promised, he now becomes a part of Grifithes group, the band of the Hawks. Within the group, Guts is instantly promoted to the leader of the vanguard and proves his competence and loyalty in his first battle. Most members of the group take a liking to him and even if of now he has not really found his purpose yet, he is happy in this new environment of his and more than useful too. Under Griffith's strategic leadership, the Band of the Hawk achieves significant military victories, notably the capture of Fort Doldrey, effectively ending the Hundred-Year War. Griffith's ambition propels him into Midland's nobility, but his manipulative tactics, including orchestrating assassinations, reveal a darker side. Guts, seeking purpose beyond being Griffith's weapon, decides to leave the Band. His departure deeply affects Griffith, leading to impulsive actions that result in his imprisonment and the near destruction of the Band. Golden Age Arc III "The Eclipse": After enduring a year of torture in his imprisonment, Griffith is rescued by Guts and remnants of the Band. However, his physical and mental state is shattered. In despair, he activates the Behelit, summoning the god Hand who initiate the Eclipse—a horrific sacrificial ceremony. During the Eclipse, Griffith sacrifices the band of the hawk to ascend as Femto, a member of the God Hand. He was too hurt after the torture to even say a word, his dream could never become a reality and when offered a second chance, he took a single look back before sacrificing his comrades and friends in the name of his ambition. The Band is annihilated by demonic Apostles, and Casca suffers a traumatic assault by Femto. Guts loses an arm and an eye attempting to save her. They are the only two members rescued by the enigmatic Skull Knight, but the ordeal leaves indelible scars.

Golden Age Arc VI "The Brand of Sacrifice":"

The Eclipse brands Guts and Casca with the Mark of Sacrifice, attracting malevolent entities. Guts embarks on a relentless quest for vengeance against Griffith and the God Hand, while also striving to protect and restore Casca's shattered psyche. This arc profoundly impacts the narrative, setting the stage for Guts' transformation into the Black Swordsman and exploring themes of destiny, sacrifice, and the human condition.
